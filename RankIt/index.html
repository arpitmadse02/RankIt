How Does the College Ranking Work?

This platform ranks colleges using a structured, data-driven process designed to be fair, transparent, and flexible. Instead of relying on a single score, multiple factors are considered together.

1

Data Preparation

Each college is represented using important parameters such as academics, infrastructure, faculty, and location. All values are standardized so that different parameters can be compared fairly.

2

Understanding College Similarity

Many colleges are very similar in performance. The system identifies such institutions and treats them as a group instead of forcing strict separation, avoiding unfair ranking differences.

3

Parameter-wise Scoring

Each college receives a score for every parameter. Instead of simple yes/no decisions, partial scores are assigned to reflect how well a college performs in each area.

4

Importance of Parameters

Different users value different aspects of education. The platform allows assigning importance to parameters so the ranking reflects individual preferences.

5

Final Score Calculation

All parameter scores and assigned weights are combined into a final score. Colleges are then sorted based on this score to generate the final ranking list.

Ranking Parameters




Teaching, Learning & Resources
(TLR)

Measures the quality of teaching and learning environment, including student strength, faculty quality, infrastructure, financial resources, and online education support.

Research and Professional Practice
(RP)

Evaluates research output and innovation, such as publications, quality of research, patents, funded projects, and contributions to sustainable development goals.

Graduation Outcomes
(GO)

Focuses on student success, including exam performance, graduation rates, and the number of Ph.D. students completing their studies.

Outreach and Inclusivity
(OI)

Measures diversity and inclusion, considering students from different regions, gender balance, support for disadvantaged groups, and facilities for differently-abled students.

Peer Perception
(PR)

Reflects reputation and public opinion based on feedback from academic peers and employers.
